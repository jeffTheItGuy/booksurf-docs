# How-To Guides

Step-by-step instructions for using Booksurf's key features.

---

## How to Create Folders

Folders are the foundation of organizing your bookmarks in Booksurf.

1. **Navigate to Your Dashboard** — Log in and go to your main dashboard or profile page.
2. **Click "Create Folder" or "New Folder"** — Look for the button in your folder manager or toolbar.
3. **Enter Folder Details:**
   - **Title:** Give your folder a clear, descriptive name
   - **Description:** Add context about what this folder contains
   - **Tags:** Add relevant keywords for easier discovery
4. **Save Your Folder** — Click "Create" or "Save" to complete the process.

> **Tip:** Once the folder is created, click the globe icon 🌐 to request to make it public.

---

## How to Add Bookmarks

Add links to your folders to build your collection of web resources.

1. **Open a Folder** — Click on the folder where you want to add a bookmark.
2. **Click "Add Bookmark"** — Look for the "+" button or "Add Bookmark" option within the folder.
3. **Enter the URL** — Paste the complete web address (including https://).
4. **Save the Bookmark** — Click "Add" or "Save" to add it to your folder.

---

## How to Make Folders Public

Control who can see your folders by adjusting privacy settings.

**Understanding Privacy Options:**
- 🔒 **Private:** Only you can see and access this folder
- 🌐 **Public:** Anyone can discover and view this folder

**Steps to make a folder public:**

1. First create the folder using the modal or Booksurf Manager.
2. Navigate to My Folders tab, Private Folder section.
3. Locate the folder you want and click on the globe icon "🌐".
4. Confirm your action in the Request Public Modal.
5. Wait for moderation — this usually takes up to 24 hours — and your folder will be made public.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Once you make a folder public, it may be indexed by search engines and discovered by other users. Make sure you're comfortable sharing the content before making it public.

## How to Use Semantic Search Effectively

Semantic search uses AI to understand the meaning of your query, not just keywords.

Unlike traditional search that matches exact words, semantic search understands context and meaning. You can search for "machine learning tutorials" and find folders about "AI education" or "neural network guides."

**How to Search:**

1. **Go to the Search Bar** — Look for the search bar in the main navigation.
2. **Enter Your Query** — Type what you're looking for in natural language.
3. **Review Results** — Browse through folders that match your query's meaning.

**Search Tips:**
- **Be descriptive:** "resources for learning web development" works better than just "code"
- **Use natural language:** Write like you're asking a person, not a computer
- **Try different phrasings:** If you don't find what you want, rephrase your query
- **Use concepts, not just keywords:** Search for "productivity tools" instead of specific app names

**Example Searches:**
- "best photography inspiration" → finds art, photo galleries, creative portfolios
- "startup resources for founders" → finds business tools, funding guides, entrepreneurship content
- "cooking recipes healthy meals" → finds nutrition sites, meal prep guides, recipe collections
